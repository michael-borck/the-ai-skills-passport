# Do I Need the Best Model?

## The Short Answer

No. Not for most of what you're doing. And the reason comes down to a distinction you may already know: **Conversation vs Delegation**.

If you're **delegating** — handing AI a task and expecting a polished result — then yes, model quality matters. Precision matters. You need the output to be right the first time, because you're not planning to iterate. In that mindset, the gap between free-tier and premium feels enormous.

If you're **conversing** — thinking with AI, exploring ideas, iterating through drafts — then a smaller model that's wrong sometimes is still useful. You're not extracting answers. You're exploring. The back-and-forth corrects for the model's weaknesses because *you're in the loop doing the thinking*.

The hardware question and the pedagogical question are the same argument from different angles.

---

## Free-Tier Tools Are Enough for Conversation

For Conversation-style use, free-tier AI tools are genuinely capable:

- **Meta AI** — free, no login friction, solid for brainstorming and iterating
- **Microsoft Copilot** — available through Curtin's Microsoft 365 agreement
- **ChatGPT (free tier)** — capable enough for most exploratory work
- **Claude (free tier)** — strong reasoning, good for structured thinking
- **Google Gemini** — free tier with competitive capability

These tools can brainstorm with you, challenge your assumptions, help you iterate on a draft, and push your thinking further. That's what Conversation requires. The model doesn't need to be perfect for that purpose.

---

## When Premium Tools Are Justified

Premium subscriptions (Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, etc.) buy you:

- **Longer context windows** — sustain a complex conversation across many turns without the model losing track
- **Fewer interruptions** — no rate limits breaking your flow mid-thought
- **Better reasoning on complex tasks** — multi-step problems, nuanced analysis, sustained iteration
- **Faster responses** — less waiting, more momentum in the back-and-forth

Notice what these are: they're all **conversation quality improvements**. They make the back-and-forth richer and longer. They don't change the fundamental dynamic — you're still doing the thinking, still making the decisions, still iterating. The quality of the conversation partner raises the ceiling on what you can explore, not what gets done for you.

This is a **workflow question, not a status question**. Premium tools are justified when your work involves sustained, complex thinking sessions — not because better tools produce better people.

---

## "It's Fine for You — You're on the Max Plan"

You'll hear this. Here are honest, defensible responses:

**The full version:**

"The subscription buys a better conversation partner, not a better answer machine. I'm still doing the thinking — the quality of the back-and-forth just raises the ceiling on what I can explore. The approach is the same on free tier; the context window is just shorter."

**The concise version:**

"Conversation amplifies thinking. A better model amplifies more. But the amplification only works if you're thinking in the first place — and that's what matters most."

Both responses reframe the challenge back to the pedagogical point without being defensive. The tool doesn't define the approach. The approach defines the approach. You can delegate on a premium plan and converse on a free one. The constraint is cognitive, not commercial.

---

## Be Transparent About What You Use

If you use premium AI tools in your own work but ask students or colleagues to use free-tier tools, own that openly. Not as guilt, but as a teaching moment:

- **Here's what I use and why** — sustained iteration, long context, complex multi-step work
- **Here's what I'm asking you to use and why** — capable enough for Conversation-style exploration, no cost barrier
- **Here's what the difference means** — I can hold longer conversations before the model loses context; the core skills are the same

This transparency is more valuable than pretending you're on the same tools. It models the kind of honest, reflective practice you're asking others to develop.

---

## The Reframe

The "must have the best frontier model" assumption is downstream of the Delegation mindset. If you're delegating, you need precision. If you're conversing, you need a willing thinking partner — and free-tier models are willing enough.

**The anxiety that "I can't afford the good tools" dissolves when you realise what "good enough" actually means for the task at hand.** For exploring ideas, challenging your thinking, iterating on drafts, and developing your own judgement? Free-tier AI is more than good enough. It's genuinely powerful.

Start with what's available. The approach matters more than the subscription.
