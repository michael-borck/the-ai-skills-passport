# AI-Proof Your Assessments — Choose Your Pathway

*Experience 4 | Assessment Design | 45-75 minutes | Teaching-focused*

---

Activity 2

## Choose Your Pathway

There's no single solution to AI-proofing assessments. Instead, consider three complementary approaches. Click each card to explore how it works and when to use it.

### Pathway A: Process Documentation

Show the journey, not just the destination

▼

Instead of just submitting a final product, students submit the raw AI conversation alongside their work. The transcript *is* the evidence. You can see whether they challenged assumptions, asked follow-up questions, or just said "do this for me" and copied the result.

**What students submit:**

- Full AI conversation transcripts (not screenshots of selected prompts)

- Brief annotations pointing to moments where they steered, challenged, or refined the AI's output

- Evidence of iteration: more prompts means a richer transcript, not a rule violation

**Why not reflection?**
AI writes excellent reflections. A student can ask AI to critique its own output and produce a convincing "reflection" without any thinking happening. The conversation transcript is much harder to fake, and the thinking is visible in the interaction pattern itself.

**Best for:**
Written assignments, reports, case analyses, any task where the thinking process matters as much as the output.

**Example transformation:**
"Write a marketing plan" becomes "Submit your marketing plan along with your full AI conversation. Use AI as a thinking partner: ask it to challenge your assumptions, suggest factors you haven't considered, or debate whether your recommendations are realistic. I'm looking for conversation, not delegation."

### Pathway B: In-Class Components

Demonstrate understanding in real-time

▼

Add live components where students must demonstrate understanding without AI assistance, not as "gotcha" tests, but as opportunities to show mastery.

**Formats that work:**

- **Viva voce:** 10-15 minute conversation about their submission

- **Real-time synthesis:** Apply their work to a new scenario in class

- **Oral defense:** Present findings and respond to questions

- **Peer review discussion:** Explain and defend choices to classmates

**Best for:**
High-stakes assessments, verifying understanding of complex submissions, developing professional communication skills.

**Example transformation:**
"Submit a business case" becomes "Submit your business case, then present the key recommendation in a 5-minute presentation where you'll respond to questions about your analysis."

### Pathway C: Personal Application

Connect to unique contexts AI cannot know

▼

Design tasks that require information only the student has: their experiences, their data, their specific context. AI can help, but cannot replace the personal element.

**Elements that create uniqueness:**

- Specific local organizations or contexts

- Primary data the student collects themselves

- Personal work or volunteer experiences

- Connection to in-class discussions or activities

**Best for:**
Developing professional identity, connecting theory to practice, capstone projects, applied learning.

**Example transformation:**
"Analyse marketing strategy" becomes "Interview someone at an organization you're connected to about their marketing challenges, then develop recommendations using concepts from weeks 3-5."

**Combining pathways:**
These approaches work together. A strong AI-resilient assessment might combine personal application (Pathway C) with process documentation (Pathway A) and a brief oral component (Pathway B). Start with one, then layer as needed.
