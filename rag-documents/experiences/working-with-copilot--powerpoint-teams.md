## PowerPoint and Teams

### Copilot in PowerPoint

Copilot can create presentations from briefs, Word documents, or a description of what you need. It generates slides with content and suggests design improvements.

**Create from a document**
Reference a Word document or brief on SharePoint and ask Copilot to turn it into a slide deck. It will organise content into slides with headings and key points.

**Add and organise slides**
Ask Copilot to add a summary slide, reorganise your deck, or create an agenda slide based on existing content.

**Design suggestions**
Copilot can suggest layout improvements, add speaker notes, or convert text-heavy slides into visual formats.

### Try this prompt

"Create a 10-slide presentation from /[Word document on SharePoint]. Keep it professional, with one key message per slide."

"Add speaker notes to each slide summarising the main talking point in two sentences."

### Copilot in Teams

Copilot in Teams shines during and after meetings. It can summarise discussions, track action items, and help you catch up on chat threads you missed.

**Meeting summaries**
After a recorded Teams meeting, ask Copilot for a summary. It identifies key discussion points, decisions made, and action items with owners.

**Catch up on chat threads**
In a busy Teams channel, ask Copilot to summarise what was discussed in the last day or week. It highlights key messages and decisions.

**Action items**
Ask Copilot to list all action items from a meeting, grouped by person. Useful for writing up minutes or following up afterwards.

### Try this prompt

"Summarise this meeting. List all decisions made and action items with the person responsible for each."

"What were the main topics discussed in this channel over the past week?"

⚠️

**What to watch for**

- PowerPoint slides from Copilot are a starting point. You will need to adjust layouts, add images, and apply your school or university template.

- Teams meeting summaries require the meeting to be recorded and transcribed. Check with attendees before recording.

- Copilot may attribute action items to the wrong person if names are unclear in the transcript. Always verify.
