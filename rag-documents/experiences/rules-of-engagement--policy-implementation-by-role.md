# Rules of Engagement — Policy Implementation by Role

*Experience 3 | Policy & Governance | 30-120 minutes | All roles*

---

In Your Context

## Policy Implementation by Role

### Unit Outline AI Statements

Your unit outline needs a clear AI policy statement. Here are templates to adapt:

Template 1: Learning-Focused

"In this unit, you will learn to use AI tools professionally and ethically. AI may be used for [specific tasks] with appropriate acknowledgment. All submitted work must demonstrate your understanding of the material. You should be able to explain and extend any work you submit."

Template 2: Task-Specific

"AI policies vary by assessment in this unit. Each assignment will specify whether AI use is permitted, required, or prohibited, and what disclosure is needed. When in doubt, ask before the due date."

First Day Communication

Consider discussing AI use explicitly in your first class. Normalize asking questions. Share your own AI use. Set the tone that transparency matters more than perfection.

### Research Group AI Guidelines

Research groups benefit from explicit conversations about AI. Consider developing guidelines that address:

**Data & Privacy:**
What data can be input to AI tools? How do you handle confidential or pre-publication information?

**Authorship & Attribution:**
How should AI assistance be acknowledged in publications? What do target journals require?

**Quality Assurance:**
How do you verify AI-assisted analysis? What human review processes are required?

**HDR Students:**
What AI use is appropriate for thesis work? How should it be documented and disclosed?

Journal Policies Are Evolving

Check target journals before submission. Many now require AI disclosure statements. Some prohibit AI for certain tasks. Build disclosure into your workflow from the start.

### Office AI Procedure Protocols

Administrative teams can benefit from shared protocols about AI use. Consider:

**Appropriate Tasks:**
Drafting routine communications, summarising documents, creating templates, scheduling assistance.

**Review Requirements:**
All AI-assisted communications should be reviewed before sending. Check facts, tone, and appropriateness.

**Data Restrictions:**
Never input confidential information, personal data, or sensitive institutional information into public AI tools.

**Accountability:**
The person sending a communication is responsible for its content, regardless of AI assistance.

Human-in-the-Loop

AI can draft, but humans decide. Any communication, decision, or document that affects people should have meaningful human review, not just rubber-stamping.
