## Amplify Your Thinking, Not the Hallucinations

AI makes it easy to chain tasks: take the output from step one and feed it into step two, then step three, and so on. It feels efficient. But there is a hidden cost: if the AI hallucinates in step one, every subsequent step builds on that error &mdash; and makes it harder to spot.

&#x26A0;&#xFE0F; How errors compound in unchecked chains

1

Research summary

AI summarises a research area but invents a study: "Smith & Chen (2024) found a 40% increase in student engagement using gamified assessments." This study does not exist.

&#x25BC;

2

Literature review draft

The fabricated study becomes a cited source. The review now treats the "40% increase" as established fact and positions it as a key finding in the field.

&#x25BC;

3

Proposal framework

The proposal uses the fabricated finding as justification: "Building on the established 40% engagement increase (Smith & Chen, 2024), we propose..." The hallucination is now a foundation.

&#x25BC;

4

Executive summary

The summary presents everything with confidence: "Extensive research demonstrates significant improvements..." The fabrication is now buried under layers of legitimate-sounding text. You present it to the committee.

*Click each step to reveal how the error grows.*

&#x2705; How conversation prevents this

&#x25B6;
**Every iteration is a checkpoint.** When you push back at each stage, you catch errors before they become foundations. "Does this study actually exist?" is a question you ask in the iterate stage.

&#x25B6;
**Every pushback is a correction.** The conversation loop surfaces assumptions. When you say "Show me where this comes from" or "That doesn't match what I've read," you interrupt the error chain before it compounds.

&#x25B6;
**Amplification is verification.** When you make the output yours, you are forced to engage with every claim. Fabrications do not survive contact with someone who actually knows the field.

"You can chain tasks and amplify the hallucinations. Or you can stay in the conversation and amplify your thinking."

### Scenario: Spot the Chain Failure

A colleague used AI to create a five-step policy brief. Read the chain below and identify where the conversation loop would have caught the problem.

STEP 1: DATA GATHERING

"Summarise the key policies on AI use in Australian higher education." &mdash; AI produces a summary that includes an "TEQSA AI Framework 2024" which does not exist.

STEP 2: ANALYSIS

"Compare these policies and identify gaps." &mdash; AI contrasts the real policies with the fabricated framework, finding "gaps" that do not exist.

STEP 3: RECOMMENDATIONS

"Draft recommendations to address these gaps." &mdash; Recommendations now address fictitious regulatory shortcomings.

STEP 4: BRIEF

"Write a one-page policy brief for leadership." &mdash; The brief confidently references the non-existent framework and recommends alignment with it.

STEP 5: PRESENTATION

"Create talking points for a meeting with the Dean." &mdash; You stand in front of the Dean citing a framework that does not exist.
