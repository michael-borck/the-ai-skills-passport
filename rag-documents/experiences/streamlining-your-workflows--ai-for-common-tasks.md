## AI for Common Tasks

Here are practical examples of AI applied to the tasks professional staff do every day. Each section includes a template prompt you can copy and adapt. Click each category to expand it.

The difference a good prompt makes

Vague prompt

"Write a reply to this email."

Generic, misses tone, may not address the real issue.

Conversation-loop prompt

"I need to respond to a stakeholder who's unhappy about a delayed timeline. Key points: the delay is due to a vendor issue, we have a new target date of March 30, and we want to maintain the relationship. Tone: professional but empathetic. Draft a response, then tell me what you assumed about the situation."

Specific context, clear tone, and asks AI to surface its assumptions so you can correct them.

&#128203; Template prompt &mdash; Try it now

I need to respond to [describe email]. The key points to address are [list]. Tone should be [professional/firm/warm]. Draft a response, then tell me what you assumed about the situation.

Copy this prompt, fill in the blanks, and paste it into your AI tool of choice.

One of the highest-value uses of AI: paste your rough notes from a meeting and get structured minutes in seconds. You still need to check accuracy, but the formatting and organisation work is done.

**How it works:**

- Take rough notes during the meeting (dot points, fragments, whatever works)

- Paste them into your AI tool with the template prompt below

- Review the output &mdash; fix names, check action items, verify decisions

- Share or file the polished version

&#128203; Template prompt &mdash; Try it now

Here are my rough notes from [meeting name/date]. Summarise into: attendees, key decisions, action items with owners, and next steps. Flag anything that seems unclear or contradictory.

Tip: If you have a Teams recording transcript, you can paste that in too. Ask AI to pull out just the decisions and actions.

AI is better at refining your existing work than generating from scratch. Use it to restructure, tighten, and identify gaps &mdash; not to write your documents for you.

&#9888;&#65039;

**Important: Start with your own content**
Never ask AI to write a report from nothing. Write your rough draft first &mdash; even if it is messy &mdash; then use AI to help you improve it. You know the context; AI does not.

&#128203; Template prompt &mdash; Try it now

Here is a draft of [document type]. Help me: 1) tighten the language, 2) identify any gaps in logic, 3) suggest a clearer structure. Do not rewrite it &mdash; give me specific suggestions I can act on.

This approach keeps you in control of the content while using AI for the editing grunt work.

Every team has processes that live in one person's head. AI is excellent at turning "the way we've always done it" into clear, written procedures &mdash; especially when you describe the process conversationally and let AI structure it.

**Why this matters:**

- Reduces "bus factor" &mdash; if that person is away, work still gets done

- Saves time training new team members

- Surfaces steps you have been doing on autopilot (and might want to streamline)

&#128203; Template prompt &mdash; Try it now

I'm going to describe a process step by step. Turn it into a clear, numbered procedure document that someone new to the role could follow. Ask me clarifying questions before you start writing.

The magic here is the last line: "Ask me clarifying questions before you start writing." This turns a one-shot prompt into a conversation that produces a much better result.

You do not need to be a spreadsheet wizard. AI can explain formulas, help you clean messy data, and describe how to build pivot tables &mdash; in plain language.

**Common use cases:**

- **Formula help:** "How do I write a VLOOKUP to match student IDs across two sheets?"

- **Data cleaning:** "I have dates in three different formats. How do I standardise them?"

- **Pivot tables:** "I need to summarise enrolments by unit and semester. Walk me through a pivot table."

- **Conditional formatting:** "Highlight rows where the budget variance is more than 10%."

&#128203; Template prompt &mdash; Try it now

I have a spreadsheet with [describe columns]. I need to [describe goal]. What formula or approach would work? Explain it so I understand the logic, not just the answer.

The "explain the logic" part matters &mdash; next time you face a similar problem, you will know how to solve it yourself.
