# Is This AI? — Detection Strategies for Your Role

*Experience 1 | Detection & Awareness | 15-60 minutes | All roles*

---

In Your Context

## Detection Strategies for Your Role

### Student Submission Scenarios

**Scenario:** A student submits work that seems inconsistent with their previous submissions. The writing is suddenly more sophisticated and uses terminology you haven't covered in class.

**Better than detection tools:**

- **Oral discussion:** Ask them to explain their argument in their own words

- **Follow-up questions:** "Can you tell me more about this point you made?"

- **Process evidence:** Request drafts, notes, or sources used

- **In-class synthesis:** Have them build on the submission in class

**Remember:** The goal isn't to catch cheaters. It's to assess learning. Design assessments that require demonstration of understanding rather than just production of text.

### AI in Research Outputs

**Consider:** As a reviewer, supervisor, or collaborator, how do you evaluate work that may have AI assistance?

**Key questions to ask:**

- Can the author defend and extend their arguments in discussion?

- Is the methodology appropriate and properly executed?

- Are claims supported by verifiable evidence?

- Does the work show original thinking or just synthesis?

**For HDR supervision:**

- Establish clear expectations about AI use early

- Focus supervision conversations on understanding, not just output

- Consider requiring process documentation (drafts, revision notes)

- Discuss AI use openly as part of research methods training

### AI-Assisted Communications

**Reality check:** Many professional communications now involve AI assistance. The question isn't "is this AI?" but "is this accurate, appropriate, and fit for purpose?"

**What matters more than detection:**

- **Accuracy:** Are facts, dates, and details correct?

- **Appropriateness:** Is the tone right for the audience?

- **Accountability:** Can the sender stand behind the content?

- **Context:** Does it show understanding of the specific situation?

**Practical approach:** Rather than trying to detect AI in communications you receive, focus on the quality and accuracy of the content. If something seems off, ask clarifying questions.
