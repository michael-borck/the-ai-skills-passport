## Data and Analysis

AI can assist with coding tasks (R, Python, SPSS syntax), data exploration, pattern spotting, and visualisation. It works best when you understand the analysis conceptually and use AI to speed up the implementation.

### How AI supports data work

**Code generation and debugging**
Describe what you want to do ("run a mixed-effects model with random intercepts for participant") and AI can generate R or Python code. It can also explain error messages and suggest fixes.

**Data exploration**
Describe your dataset structure and ask AI to suggest initial exploratory analyses: distributions to check, correlations to examine, potential confounds to consider.

**Visualisation**
Ask AI to generate ggplot2, matplotlib, or other plotting code. Describe the chart you want and the variables involved, and it will produce publication-ready code.

**SPSS syntax**
If you use SPSS, AI can generate syntax for complex procedures, saving you from navigating menus. Describe the analysis in plain language and get syntax you can paste directly.

### Try this prompt

Describe a dataset you are working with (or a hypothetical one) and try this prompt in ChatGPT or Claude:

"I have a dataset with [describe variables, sample size, and structure]. I want to examine [your research question]. Suggest three appropriate statistical analyses, explain why each is suitable, and note any assumptions I should check first."

Evaluate the suggestions against your own statistical knowledge. Did AI recommend anything inappropriate? Did it surface an approach you had not considered?

⚠️

**What to watch for**

- AI-generated code can contain subtle bugs (wrong variable names, incorrect join types, off-by-one errors). Always test with known outputs.

- AI may suggest analyses that are technically correct but inappropriate for your research design or data structure.

- Never upload sensitive or identifiable research data to public AI tools. Use anonymised or synthetic data for prompting, or use institutionally approved tools.

- AI cannot replace your understanding of why an analysis is appropriate. You need to defend your methods in peer review.
