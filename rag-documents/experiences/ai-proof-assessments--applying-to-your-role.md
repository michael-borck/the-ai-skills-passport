# AI-Proof Your Assessments — Applying to Your Role

*Experience 4 | Assessment Design | 45-75 minutes | Teaching-focused*

---

In Your Context

## Applying These Ideas to Your Role

### Assessment Redesign Priorities

**The mindset shift:**
Move from "How do I stop students using AI?" to "How do I assess what students can actually do?"

**Quick wins for next semester:**

- Ask students to submit full AI conversation transcripts alongside their work

- Frame the expectation upfront: "I'm looking for conversation, not delegation"

- Include one in-class component (even a brief Q&A) for major submissions

- Require connection to a specific example from class discussion

- Update rubrics to value reasoning and iteration over polish

**Sample syllabus statement:**
"AI tools may be used as a thinking partner. Submit your full AI conversation alongside your work. I'm looking for evidence that you engaged in genuine conversation with AI, not that you delegated the task. Good conversations involve follow-up questions, challenges, and refinement."

### HDR Supervision in an AI Era

**The challenge:**
How do you verify a research student's understanding when drafts might be AI-assisted?

**Process-focused supervision:**

- Require verbal explanation of written work in supervision meetings

- Ask students to walk you through their literature review choices

- Have them defend methodology decisions without referring to documents

- Discuss limitations and alternatives to assess depth of understanding

**Establishing norms early:**
"In our supervisions, I'll ask you to explain your work verbally. This isn't about catching AI use. It's about ensuring you're developing the research skills you'll need for your career."

### Evaluating AI-Assisted Work

**The reality:**
Staff using AI to draft reports, emails, and documents is now common. The question is quality control, not detection.

**What to look for in submissions/reports:**

- **Accuracy:** Are facts and figures verifiable?

- **Context:** Does it show understanding of our specific situation?

- **Accountability:** Can the author answer questions about content?

- **Judgment:** Are recommendations appropriate for our context?

**Practical approach:**
Ask clarifying questions about key decisions or recommendations. If someone can explain and defend their work, the tool used to draft it matters less than the quality of thinking behind it.
