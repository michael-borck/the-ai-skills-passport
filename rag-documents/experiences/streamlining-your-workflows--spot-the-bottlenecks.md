## Spot the Bottlenecks

Before you can streamline anything, you need to know where your time actually goes. This quick audit helps you identify the repetitive tasks that are prime candidates for AI assistance.

### Time Audit: Where does your time go?

Select the tasks that take up a significant chunk of your week. Pick your **top 3&ndash;5 time sinks**.

**Drafting emails and correspondence**
Composing, replying, following up

**Summarising meeting notes or minutes**
Turning messy notes into structured records

**Writing or updating reports**
Recurring reports, status updates, briefs

**Formatting documents and spreadsheets**
Making things look right, consistent layouts

**Scheduling and coordination**
Finding times, booking rooms, chasing RSVPs

**Answering recurring questions from colleagues**
The same questions, different people, every week

**Compiling data from multiple sources**
Pulling numbers together, cross-referencing systems

**Reviewing and editing documents**
Proofreading, consistency checks, formatting fixes

**Creating agendas, briefs, or summaries**
Preparing documents for meetings and decisions

**Process documentation and how-to guides**
Writing up procedures so others can follow them

&#128161;

**Key insight**
Not every task is worth automating. The sweet spot is tasks that are *repetitive*, *structured*, and *low-risk* &mdash; where a mistake is easy to catch and fix.

### The AI Suitability Matrix

Where do your selected tasks fall?

High Frequency + Low Complexity

**Perfect for AI**
Emails, summaries, formatting, standard replies. Delegate these confidently and review the output.

High Frequency + High Complexity

**AI as Thinking Partner**
Reports, analysis, stakeholder comms. Use AI to draft, challenge, and refine &mdash; but you drive.

Low Frequency + Low Complexity

**Probably Not Worth It**
One-off simple tasks. The time spent setting up the prompt exceeds the time saved.

Low Frequency + High Complexity

**Use AI Carefully**
Annual reports, policy drafts, complex processes. AI can help, but verify everything. High stakes mean higher scrutiny.
