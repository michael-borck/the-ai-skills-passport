## Copilot in Excel

Copilot in Excel lets you ask questions about your data in plain English, generate formulas without memorising syntax, and create charts with a simple description of what you want to see.

### What Copilot can do in Excel

**Ask questions about your data**
Type a question like "What was the total spend in Q3?" or "Which department had the highest enrolment?" Copilot analyses your spreadsheet and gives you the answer.

**Generate formulas**
Describe what you need in plain language and Copilot writes the formula. No more searching for VLOOKUP syntax or nested IF statements.

**Create charts and visualisations**
Ask Copilot to create a chart from your data. Describe the type you want (bar, line, pie) and what it should show.

**Highlight and sort data**
Ask Copilot to highlight trends, flag outliers, or sort data by specific criteria. Useful for quick analysis before a meeting.

### Try this prompt

Open a spreadsheet with data (enrolment numbers, budget figures, or similar), click the Copilot icon, and try:

"Summarise the key trends in this data. What are the highest and lowest values, and are there any notable patterns?"

To generate a formula:

"Add a column that calculates the percentage change between this year and last year for each row."

To create a chart:

"Create a bar chart comparing enrolment numbers by department for the last three semesters."

⚠️

**What to watch for**

- Your data needs to be in a formatted table for Copilot to work well. Select your data and click "Format as Table" first.

- Copilot can make calculation errors. Spot-check any formulas it generates against known values.

- Complex multi-sheet workbooks can confuse Copilot. Start with a single, clean data table for best results.
