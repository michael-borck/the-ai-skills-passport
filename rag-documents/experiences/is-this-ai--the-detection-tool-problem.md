## The Detection Tool Problem

⚠️

**The uncomfortable truth about AI detectors:**
Even the best AI detection tools have significant accuracy problems. They produce false positives (flagging human writing as AI) and false negatives (missing actual AI content) at rates that make them unreliable for high-stakes decisions.

### Research findings on detection accuracy:

**False positive rates:** 20-40% of human-written text incorrectly flagged as AI

**Non-native speakers:** ESL writers flagged at significantly higher rates

**Formal writing:** Academic style more likely to be flagged than casual writing

**Easy evasion:** Simple paraphrasing or asking AI to "write more naturally" defeats most detectors

### What this means for you:

- Never use detection tools as the sole evidence for academic misconduct

- Detection results should prompt conversation, not accusation

- Focus on assessing understanding rather than detecting AI

- Consider process-based assessment approaches
