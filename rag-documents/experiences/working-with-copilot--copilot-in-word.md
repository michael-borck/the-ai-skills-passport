## Copilot in Word

Copilot in Word helps you draft documents from scratch, summarise existing ones, rewrite sections for a different audience, and pull content from files on SharePoint or OneDrive.

### What Copilot can do in Word

**Draft from notes or briefs**
Give Copilot dot points, meeting notes, or a rough outline and ask it to produce a structured draft. Great for committee papers, reports, or proposals.

**Summarise long documents**
Open a lengthy policy document or report and ask Copilot to summarise key points. Useful for preparing briefs or catching up on reading.

**Rewrite for tone or audience**
Ask Copilot to rewrite a section to be more concise, more formal, or more accessible. Helpful when adapting content for different stakeholders.

**Reference SharePoint content**
Use the "/" command to reference files stored on SharePoint or OneDrive. Copilot can incorporate content from those files into your current document.

### Try this: Delegation (fine for routine tasks)

Open a new Word document, click the Copilot icon, and try:

"Draft a one-page committee brief summarising the key points from /[SharePoint file name]. Include a recommendation section at the end."

This is delegation — Copilot does the work, you review. Perfectly fine for routine summaries and first drafts.

### Now try this: Conversation (for work that matters)

When the document needs your thinking — a proposal, a recommendation, a case for change — try a different approach:

"I'm writing a brief recommending we [your proposal]. Before I draft it, what are the three strongest objections a sceptical reader might raise? And what evidence would I need to address each one?"

Then follow up:

"Good. Now help me structure the brief so the recommendation feels inevitable by the time the reader reaches it. What should come first — the problem, the evidence, or the cost of inaction?"

Notice the difference: you are thinking *with* Copilot, not handing the task over. The document that results will be yours, shaped by your judgement, not a generic AI draft.

⚠️

**What to watch for**

- Copilot may invent details that were not in your source material. Always verify facts and figures.

- Referenced files need to be on SharePoint or OneDrive (not your local desktop) for Copilot to access them.

- The output is a starting point. Your knowledge of the context, stakeholders, and politics is what makes it fit for purpose.
