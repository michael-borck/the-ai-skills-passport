## The Loop: Brainstorm &rarr; Ideate &rarr; Iterate &rarr; Amplify

Most people treat AI as a vending machine: insert prompt, receive output, walk away. The conversation loop is different. It keeps you thinking at every stage.

&#x1F9E0;

BRAINSTORM

Define your question

->

&#x1F4A1;

IDEATE

Go wide

->

&#x1F504;

ITERATE

Push back

->

&#x1F680;

AMPLIFY

Make it yours

&#x21BB; REPEAT until your thinking has landed

&#x1F9E0; Brainstorm &mdash; Define your real question

Before you type a single prompt, ask yourself: *Am I describing my problem, or my attempted fix?* Most people skip straight to "Write me a..." when the real question is something deeper. A good brainstorm starts with what you actually need to figure out.

**Instead of:** "Write me a rubric for my marketing unit."

**Try:** "I'm struggling to assess whether students understand segmentation or are just repeating textbook definitions. How might I design assessment criteria that surface real understanding?"

&#x1F4A1; Ideate &mdash; Go wide

Ask for alternatives, angles you hadn't considered. Resist stopping at the first interesting output. The first response from AI is almost never the best one &mdash; it is the most average one. Push for variety: "What are three completely different ways to approach this?" or "What am I probably not thinking about?"

**The trap:** The first AI response sounds reasonable, so you stop. But reasonable is not the same as right. Keep going.

&#x1F504; Iterate &mdash; Push back

Refine. Redirect. Challenge. This is where conversation actually happens. Say things like: *"That's closer, but..."* / *"The tone is wrong for this audience"* / *"You're solving the wrong problem."* Every pushback sharpens the output and sharpens your own thinking.

**Remember:** AI does not know your context, your students, your colleagues, or your standards. Only you do. Each iteration adds what the AI is missing.

&#x1F680; Amplify &mdash; Make it yours

Edit, restructure, bring your voice, your context, your standards. The question to ask yourself: *Would I put my name on this?* If not, you are not done. Amplify means the final output reflects your expertise, not the AI's averaging.

**The test:** If someone asked you to explain or defend what you produced, could you do it with confidence? That is the sign it is yours.

&#x21BB; Repeat &mdash; Loop until your thinking has landed

Most good work loops more than once. The sign you are done is not that the AI has stopped generating &mdash; it is that *your thinking has landed*. You know what you think. You can articulate it. The AI helped you get there, but the thinking is yours.

### Delegation vs Conversation

Click a tab to see the same task done two ways: delegation (one prompt, accept output) versus conversation (loop through all four stages).

&#x274C; DELEGATION

**Task:** Create a rubric for a marketing case study.

*"Write me a rubric for a third-year marketing case study assignment."* &rarr; Accept the output. Paste it into the unit outline. Done.

Result: a generic rubric that could belong to any unit at any university.

&#x2705; CONVERSATION

**Task:** Create a rubric for a marketing case study.

**Brainstorm:** "What am I really assessing &mdash; application or recall?"
**Ideate:** "Show me three different rubric structures."
**Iterate:** "Option 2 is closest but needs a criterion for real-world data use."
**Amplify:** Edit criteria to reflect your specific unit learning outcomes, add exemplars from your own marking experience.

Result: a rubric shaped by your expertise that you can explain and defend.

&#x274C; DELEGATION

**Task:** Draft a literature review section.

*"Write a literature review on consumer behaviour in sustainable fashion."* &rarr; Accept the output. Drop it into the manuscript.

Result: plausible-sounding text with potentially fabricated references and no theoretical positioning.

&#x2705; CONVERSATION

**Task:** Draft a literature review section.

**Brainstorm:** "What gap am I actually arguing exists in the literature?"
**Ideate:** "What are the major theoretical lenses used in this space? Which debates are unresolved?"
**Iterate:** "You've missed the tension between TPB and practice theory. Reframe around that."
**Amplify:** Replace AI-suggested references with verified sources, restructure argument to support your specific contribution.

Result: a literature review that reflects your scholarly positioning and uses verified sources.

&#x274C; DELEGATION

**Task:** Write a process guide for new staff onboarding.

*"Write an onboarding checklist for new administrative staff."* &rarr; Accept the output. Save as PDF. Send to new hires.

Result: a generic checklist missing your specific systems, contacts, and institutional quirks.

&#x2705; CONVERSATION

**Task:** Write a process guide for new staff onboarding.

**Brainstorm:** "What do new staff actually struggle with in their first week?"
**Ideate:** "What formats work best &mdash; checklist, timeline, or FAQ?"
**Iterate:** "This misses the Curtin-specific systems like OASIS and StaffConnect. Add those."
**Amplify:** Add real contact names, correct links, and the unofficial tips that only experienced staff know.

Result: a genuinely useful guide that reflects institutional knowledge no AI could have.
