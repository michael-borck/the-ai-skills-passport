*Experience 5 | Co-Creation & Integration | 30-60 minutes | All roles*

## In Your Context

Different roles have different AI use cases. Explore prompts tailored to your work context.

Teaching Applications

Lecture Preparation

Example Prompt:

You are an expert in [SUBJECT] with experience teaching university students.

Create a 50-minute lecture outline on [TOPIC] that includes:
- An engaging opening hook
- 3-4 key concepts with real-world examples
- Two interactive activities (think-pair-share or similar)
- Discussion questions to check understanding
- A memorable closing that links to next week's content

Context: Second-year undergraduate students, diverse backgrounds, mixed engagement levels.

Format as a structured outline with timing for each section.

Quiz Generation

Example Prompt:

You are an assessment specialist who creates questions that test understanding, not just recall.

Create 10 multiple-choice questions on [TOPIC] at varying difficulty levels:
- 3 questions testing basic recall and definitions
- 4 questions requiring application to scenarios
- 3 questions requiring analysis or evaluation

For each question, provide:
- The question stem
- Four answer options (one correct, three plausible distractors)
- Brief explanation of why the correct answer is correct
- The Bloom's taxonomy level being assessed

Context: [COURSE LEVEL], students have completed readings on [CHAPTERS/TOPICS].

Student Feedback

Example Prompt (Few-Shot Style):

I want you to write feedback for student work in my style.

Example 1:
Student wrote: "Social media marketing is important for businesses"
My feedback: "You've identified a valid point, but this needs development. What makes it important? Can you provide specific examples or data? Push beyond the obvious."

Example 2:
Student wrote: "The campaign was successful because sales increased"
My feedback: "Good start. Now dig deeper: successful compared to what benchmark? What specifically drove the increase? Correlation isn't causation. What else might explain this?"

Now write feedback in my style for:
[PASTE STUDENT WORK]

Research Applications

Literature Review Synthesis

Example Prompt:

You are a research assistant helping me synthesize literature for a systematic review.

I will provide summaries of several papers on [TOPIC]. Please:
1. Identify common themes across the papers
2. Note methodological approaches used
3. Highlight points of agreement and disagreement
4. Identify gaps in the current literature
5. Suggest how these papers relate to my research question: [YOUR RQ]

Present this as a synthesis narrative, not a paper-by-paper summary.

[PASTE PAPER SUMMARIES]

Writing Improvement

Example Prompt (Devil's Advocate):

I'm drafting the methodology section of my paper. Please critique it as if you were Reviewer 2 having a bad day.

Identify:
1. Weaknesses in my justification for methodological choices
2. Potential validity threats I haven't addressed
3. Missing information that would concern a reviewer
4. Where my writing is unclear or assumes too much knowledge

Be specific and rigorous. I want to strengthen this before submission.

[PASTE METHODOLOGY SECTION]

Administrative Applications

Communication Drafting

Example Prompt:

You are a professional communication specialist in a university setting.

Draft an email about [TOPIC/SITUATION].

Context:
- Audience: [WHO]
- Purpose: [WHAT YOU WANT TO ACHIEVE]
- Tone: Professional but approachable
- Any sensitivities: [NOTE ANY]

The email should be concise (under 200 words) and include a clear call to action.

Draft two versions: one more formal, one warmer. I'll choose which fits better.

Meeting Preparation

Example Prompt (Board of Directors):

I'm preparing for a meeting about [TOPIC/PROPOSAL].

Please help me anticipate the discussion by analysing this from the perspectives of:
- The budget holder (financial concerns)
- The staff who will implement it (practical concerns)
- Students who will be affected (experience concerns)
- Risk and compliance (policy concerns)

For each perspective, identify:
1. Their likely questions
2. Their potential objections
3. What would make them supportive

Then suggest how I should frame my proposal to address these concerns proactively.

## Key Takeaways

RTCF is Your Foundation

Role, Task, Context, Format. These four elements transform vague requests into precise prompts that get useful results.

First Output is a Draft

Real value comes from iteration. Ask AI to improve, critique, or approach differently. The conversation is the process.

Match Technique to Task

Different challenges need different approaches. Build your toolkit of techniques and know when to deploy each one.

Build Your Library

Reusable prompts save time and improve consistency. Invest in crafting prompts for your recurring tasks.
