## Writing and Editing

AI can assist with drafting sections, paraphrasing, adjusting academic tone, and getting structural feedback on your writing. The key is knowing when AI helps and when it starts to erode your distinctive voice.

### Where AI writing assistance works well

**Overcoming blank page paralysis**
Give AI your dot points, findings, or rough notes and ask for a structured first draft. This gets words on the page that you then rewrite in your own voice.

**Paraphrasing and clarity**
Paste a paragraph and ask AI to suggest clearer phrasing, reduce word count, or simplify complex sentences without changing the meaning.

**Structural feedback**
Ask AI to evaluate the logical flow of a section: "Does my argument follow logically? Are there gaps between my evidence and my conclusion?"

**Academic tone adjustment**
If you are writing for a different audience (grant application vs journal article vs conference abstract), AI can help adjust formality and emphasis.

**Where AI writing hinders**

- **Your unique voice matters.** If reviewers know your work, a sudden shift to generic AI prose is noticeable and undermines credibility.

- **AI cannot argue for you.** It can structure an argument, but the intellectual contribution (the "so what?") must come from you.

- **Over-reliance dulls your skills.** Writing is thinking. If AI does the drafting, you may miss gaps in your own reasoning.

### Try this: Delegation vs Conversation

The difference between AI as a shortcut and AI as a thinking partner comes down to how you prompt. Try both of these with the same paragraph from your current draft and compare the results:

**Delegation prompt:**
"Rewrite this paragraph to be clearer and more academic."

You get polished text back, but you have not thought about *why* your original was unclear. The AI did the thinking for you.

**Conversation prompt:**
"Read this paragraph. What is the single weakest point in my argument? What assumption am I making that a reviewer might challenge? Do not rewrite it — just identify the problems so I can fix them myself."

You get diagnostic feedback that makes *you* a better writer. The AI is a thinking partner, not a ghostwriter.

Notice the difference? The delegation prompt produces text. The conversation prompt produces insight. After trying both, ask yourself: which version helped you understand your own writing better?

⚠️

**What to watch for**

- AI tends to make writing blander. If your text loses its edge after AI editing, the original was probably better.

- Check that AI has not introduced hedging language ("it could be argued that") where you were making a definitive claim.

- Be transparent about AI use. Most journals now require disclosure (covered in Step 5).
