# What Would You Do? — Scenarios 4-5

*Experience 2 | Ethics & Dilemmas | 20-90 minutes | All roles*

---

Activity 1 — Part 2

## The Dilemma Navigator (continued)

Two more scenarios to consider. Remember, the goal is to examine your reasoning, not to find the "right" answer.

SCENARIO 4: YOUR OWN WORK

You're writing a paper and use AI to help structure your literature review. The AI suggests sources and synthesizes themes. You verify the sources exist and are relevant. You're not sure how much to acknowledge this assistance.

What would you do?

SCENARIO 5: THE STAFF MEETING

In a school meeting, a colleague advocates for banning all AI use in assessments. Another says that's unrealistic and disadvantages students. The Head of School asks for your view.

What would you do?

## Reflection Notes

Each scenario has multiple valid perspectives. Here are considerations for each choice:

**Scenario 1:**
- Option A: Pragmatic. But consider: if AI helps craft extension requests, does it change how you evaluate sincerity?
- Option B: Reasonable middle ground without accusations. But are you applying this consistently?
- Option C: Direct, but potentially confrontational. What would you do with the answer?
- Option D: Focuses on what matters most. But if you see a pattern, should you address it?

**Scenario 2:**
- Option A: Acknowledges AI is increasingly normal. But does the level of involvement matter?
- Option B: Shows concern for process. How would you react if they said 'AI wrote most of it'?
- Option C: Proactive about transparency. Might this disadvantage your colleague?
- Option D: Strong stance. What threshold would make you comfortable?

**Scenario 3:**
- Option A: Principled consistency. But are accessibility accommodations also 'exceptions'?
- Option B: Thoughtful reframing. Where's the line between assistance and replacement?
- Option C: Humanizing approach. How do you avoid implying accusation?
- Option D: Collaborative and cautious. How might the student experience the wait?

**Scenario 4:**
- Option A: Common view, but increasingly contested. Why does AI feel different from a database?
- Option B: Balanced transparency. Does 'AI assisted' capture the involvement?
- Option C: Wisely defers to emerging norms. What if there's no policy yet?
- Option D: Cautious. Is the concern about AI, or about standing behind the work?

**Scenario 5:**
- Option A: Values clarity. But how do you enforce a ban?
- Option B: Pragmatic. How do you address colleagues feeling their expertise is devalued?
- Option C: Middle path. Does this burden individual staff to navigate ambiguity?
- Option D: Evidence-based. How do you support staff in the meantime?
